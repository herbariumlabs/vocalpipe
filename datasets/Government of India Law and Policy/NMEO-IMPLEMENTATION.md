IMPLEMENTATION OF NATIONAL FOOD SECURITY MISSION: OILSEEDS, TBOs & TRFA AND NATIONAL MISSION OF EDIBLE OILS (NMEO): OIL PALM DURING 2024-25

Government of India is implementing centrally sponsored scheme on oilseeds and oil palm development programmes to increase the production of Oilseeds to bring down the dependence of import of edible oil.

National Mission on Oilseeds & Oil Palm (NMOOP) was launched in 2014-15 and continued upto 2017-18 to augment the availability of vegetable oils and to reduce the import of edible oils by increasing the production and productivity of oilseeds (Groundnut, Soybean, Rapeseed & Mustard, Sunflower, Safflower, Sesame, Niger, Linseed and Castor) and area expansion of oil palm and Tree Borne Oilseeds (TBOs) (Olive, Mahua, Kokum, Wild Apricot, Neem, Jojoba, Karanja, Simaroba, Tung, Cheura and Jatropha) in the country The NMOOP scheme has been merged with revamped NFSM. Accordingly from 2018-19 onwards the existing NMOOP is being implemented under NFSM as NFSM-Oilseeds & Oil palm comprising the sub components NFSM-Oilseeds, NFSM-Oil Palm and NFSM-TBOs.

From 2021-22 onwards, The NMEO: Oil Palm is implemented in 14 states viz., Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Telangana, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Karnataka, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Mizoram, Nagaland, Goa and Kerala.

Financial sharing pattern:- Sharing of financial expenses between central and state Govt. in the ratio of 60:40 for general states and 90:10 for NE and Himalayan states.

Objectives NFSM-Oilseeds & NMEO- Oil Palm:

- To augment the availability of vegetable oils and to reduce the import of edible oils by increasing the production and productivity of vegetable oils sourced from Oilseeds, Oil palm and TBOs.

- To increase production and productivity of oilseeds from an average production of

  29.79 million tonnes and productivity of 1122 kg/ha obtained during 12^(th) plan period (2012-13 to 2016-17) to 36.10 million tonnes and 1290 kg/ha, respectively by end of 2019-20.

- To achieve additional area of 1.05 lakh hectare under oil palm cultivation during 2017-18 to 2019-20 area over on and above current area of 3.15 lakh ha. (2016-17). With this additional area total area of about 4.20 lakh ha, will be under oil palm cultivation by 2020.

- To cover an area of about 4800 ha under plantation of TBOs namely Olive, Mahua, Kokum, Wild Apricot, Neem, Jojoba, Karanja, Simaroba, Tung, Cheura and Jatropha, upto March, 2020.

NFSM: Oilseeds

The scheme of NFSM: Oilseeds is implemented in 29 states during 2024-25 viz., Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Haryana, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Odisha, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal,

Assam, Jammu & Kashmir, Jharkhand, Nagaland, Tripura, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Sikkim, Uttarakhand, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Puducherry, Goa & Ladakh.

NFSM-Oilseeds is implemented with three broad interventions, viz., Seed Components, Production Inputs and Transfer of Technology. The details of components under these three main interventions are given as under.

a)  Seed components - Purchase of breeder seed, Production foundation seed, Production of Certified seed, Distribution of certified seed, Seed Hubs, Infrastructure Development, Seed minikits of Oilseed crops.

b)  Production Inputs – i) Nutrient management: Distribution of Gypsum/pyrite/ Liming /SSP etc; Supply of Rhyzobium/PSB/ZSB/ Azatobacter/ Mycorrhiza culture,

  ii) Pest Management: PP Chemicals/Insecticides/Biopesticide/ weedicide /Bioagents/ micronutrients, etc; Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus (NPV), iii) Farm mechanization: Distribution of manual/power operated PP Equipments; Supply of manual/power operated Farm Implements; Seed Storage Bins; Seed Treatment Drums, iv) Irrigation management:- Distribution of Sprinkler sets; Pipes carrying water from source to field.

c)  Transfer of Technology – i) Officers training, ii) farmers training, iii) Cluster demonstration, iv) Cluster demonstrations on Bee keeping, v) Frontline demonstrations, vi) Cluster Frontline Demonstrations (CFLDs) by Krishi Vigyan Kendras and v) Farmers filed schools.

NMEO-Oil Palm:

The NMEO: Oil Palm is implemented in 15 states during 2024-25 viz., Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Telangana, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Karnataka, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, Tripura, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Mizoram, Nagaland, Goa and Kerala.

NMEO: Oil Palm is implemented with three broad interventions, viz., Area Expansion inputs components, Production Inputs and Transfer of Technology. The details of components under these three main interventions are given as under.

a)  Area Expansion inputs components – assistance for planting material, maintenance/cultivation cost, inputs for inter-cropping in oil palm fields during gestation period of 4 years, Establishment of Nursery, Replanting of Old Oil Pal garden

b)  Production Inputs– Drip-irrigation, bore-well/water harvesting structure, diesel/ electric pump set, vermi-compost unit, establishment of new seed garden, machinery & tools, special component for NE/hilly states/ Left Wing (LW) Areas for construction of roads and establishment of new oil palm processing unit and

c)  Transfer of Technology–Assistance for training of farmers/officers, Custom hiring centre cum harvester group, need based R&D projects on oil palm including import of germplasm and training infrastructure support to Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR)/State Agriculture Universities (SAUs), flexi funds/ local initiatives

NFSM: TBOs under NFSM:

The interventions under NFSM (TBOs) comprises of three major components i.e area expansion inputs component covering integrated development of nurseries and plantation of TBOs, maintenance of TBOs plantation. Production inputs covering intercropping with TBOs, transfer of technologies covers training of farmers and extension officials/workers. Besides, support is provided for need based R & D projects through institutes of ICAR/ICFRE/IITs/ SAUs and installation of pre-processing and oil expeller units, collection of TBOs. etc.

States/UTs covered under NFSM-TBOs during 2024-25

  1. Chhattisgarh, 2. Maharashtra, 3. Mizoram, 4. Rajasthan 5. Tamil Nadu 6. Tripura, 7.

  Uttar Pradesh & 8. Jammu & Kashmir

Targeting Rice Fallow Area (TRFA) in Eastern States:

The Government of India is implementing a centrally sponsored scheme on Targeting Rice Fallow Area (TRFA) in Eastern States of Assam, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand and West Bengal to bring rice fallow area under oilseed cultivation from 2016-17 onwards. The scheme is under implementation in 15 identified districts of five States. A project to cover

4.5 lakh ha area @ 1.5 lakh ha Rice fallow area every year for 3 years i.e. 2017-18 to 2019- 20 targeting in six Eastern states namely Assam, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Odisha and West Bengal. During 2019-20, 4 new states namely Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka & Tamil Nadu have been included under TRFA covering 75 districts and 7500 villages in these states with a production target of 3.50 lakh tonnes of oilseeds.

Under the scheme, assistance is given for conducting (i) Cluster Demonstration of Rabi Oilseeds viz., Mustard, Linseeds, Safflower, Sesame, Sunflower & Groundnut, (ii) Providing production inputs like Distribution of Certified seeds, micro nutrients, Gypsum, Lime, Bio- fertilizer, Bio-Pesticides, (iii) water saving devises like Sprinkler sets and water carrying pipes (iv) Farm machinery distribution , (v) Farmers and Officers Trainings.
