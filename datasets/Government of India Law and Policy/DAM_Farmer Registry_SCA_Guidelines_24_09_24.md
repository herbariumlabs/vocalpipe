1.  The Union Cabinet has approved Digital Agriculture Mission on 2nd September 2024. Agri Stack, a component of Digital Agriculture Mission, is a farmer-centric Digital Public infrastructure (DPI) being built as a public good like Aadhar for efficient, easier and faster service and scheme delivery to the farmers. It is being built in a federated structure as a collaborative project between the various agencies of the Central and State Governments and the Union Territories. It consists of three foundational registries or databases in the agriculture sector, i.e., the Farmers' Registry, GeoÂ­ referenced village maps and the Crop Sown Registry, all created and maintained by the State Governments/ Union Territories.

2.  Taking this forward, the Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (DoA&FW) signed Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with State Governments, for conducting Digital Crop Surveys, creating Farmer Registries, and including village land maps. In the implementation of DPI for Agriculture, the MoU plays an important role as it outlines Centre and State responsibilities in a collaborative framework under which States are supported with training, technical and financial assistance in the form of Reference Application and funds for building and maintenance of various building blocks of DPI. The Mou also enables a mechanism of aggregate data sharing between the Centre and the State.

3.  Farmer Registry/database is linked dynamically to State Record of Rights (RoR) system. This registry is essential for streamlining government processes pertaining to farmers, enhancing service delivery, and minimizing the duplication of benefits.   The Government of [Name of the State/UT] undertakes the following commitments:

1.  Establishment of the Farmer Registry:

  The State/UT will ensure the establishment of the State Farmer Registry, including the collection, verification, and digitization of farmer and land ownership data, as per the directions of the Ministry.

2.  Technical and Administrative Readiness:

  The necessary administrative and technical infrastructure, including the provisioning of Records of Rights (RoR) data, implementation of Farmland Plot IDs, and integration with the Unified Land API, will be established and maintained by the State/UT.

3.  Data Sharing and Integration:

  The State/UT will facilitate the sharing of required data through APls to ensure real-time updates to the Farmer Registry, in compliance with the guidelines, and integrate the registry with the RoR system for seamless and accurate records.

4.  Compliance with Guidelines:

  The State/UT will adhere to all operational, technical, and administrative guidelines prescribed by the Ministry, ensuring compliance with data privacy and security protocols, including eKYC and Aadhaar-based authentication.

5.  Long-term Sustainability:

  The State/UT will ensure the long-term sustainability of the State Farmer Registry, including the allocation of resources for its operation, maintenance, and regular updates following the go-live stage.

6.  Utilization of Funds:

  The funds provided under the SCA scheme will be utilized strictly for the implementation and maintenance of the State Farmer Registry, in accordance with the milestones and provisions outlined in the scheme guidelines.

7.  Periodic Reporting:

  The State/UT will submit periodic progress reports as required by the Ministry, detailing the number of farmers registered, utilization of funds, and progress in integration with the Agri Stack.
