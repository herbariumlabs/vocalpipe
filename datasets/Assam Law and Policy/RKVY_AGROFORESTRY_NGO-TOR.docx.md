**TERMS OF REFERENCE FOR**

**ENGAGEMENT OF NGOs FOR MONITORING AND EVALUATION UNDER RKVY AGROFORESTRY COMPONENT**

1. **Background**

The Assam Agroforestry Development Board (AADB) seeks to engage Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) specializing in climate change and environmental conservation to monitor and evaluate the progress of agroforestry initiatives under the Pradhan Mantri Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (PM-RKVY) Agroforestry Component. The objective is to ensure effective implementation, assess impact, and provide data-driven insights for informed decision-making by the Board.

2. # **Scope and Objective of the Assignment**

The selected NGOs will be responsible for monitoring agroforestry progress, assessing effectiveness, and preparing reports to support data-driven decision-making. The scope of work includes:

1. # **Monitoring Agroforestry Implementation**

   * Conduct field visits to track agroforestry plantation progress.

     * Assess plantation quality and survival rates.

     * Identify challenges and gaps in implementation.

   2. # **Data Collection and Analysis**

      * Gather field data on agroforestry interventions, including species survival, growth performance, and community participation.

      * Analyze the impact of agroforestry projects on soil health, biodiversity, and livelihoods.

      * Maintain a database of findings for comparative analysis over time.

   3. # **Evaluation and Reporting**

      * Prepare periodic progress reports on agroforestry implementation.

      * Conduct impact assessment studies to measure environmental and socioeconomic benefits.

      * Provide actionable recommendations based on data insights.

   4. # **Capacity Building and Stakeholder Engagement**

      * Engage with farmers, self-help groups (SHGs), and other stakeholders to gather feedback.

      * Conduct training programs on best practices in agroforestry monitoring.

      * Collaborate with government agencies and technical experts to enhance project outcomes.

3. # **Coordination and Decision-Making**

The selected NGOs will work under AADB's supervision. All reports and evaluations will be reviewed by the Board to guide decision-making and improve agroforestry initiatives.

4. # **Location and Duration of Assignment**

The assignment will cover various districts implementing the RKVY Agroforestry Component. Engagement duration will be determined based on project needs and performance evaluations.

5. # **Expected Outputs**

* Regular monitoring reports detailing agroforestry progress, survival rates, and implementation challenges.

* Comprehensive	evaluation	reports	providing	insights	into	the	environmental	and socioeconomic impact of agroforestry projects.

* Data-driven recommendations to enhance decision-making and improve agroforestry outcomes.

* Capacity-building programs to strengthen community-level monitoring capabilities.

7. **Final Selection**

NGOs must achieve a minimum score of 60 points to qualify for further consideration. Only those that meet or exceed this threshold will be eligible for the next stage of evaluation. The highest-scoring NGOs will be shortlisted based on their overall performance, with priority given to those that demonstrate exceptional impact, efficiency, and alignment with the organization’s objectives. Shortlisted NGOs will undergo a more detailed review, which may include additional assessments, interviews, or site visits, before a final decision is made regarding their engagement. The NGO must apply as per the enclosed standard application form (Annexure-I).
