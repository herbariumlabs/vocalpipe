1. # **Introduction :**

   1. Industries, Commerce & Public Enterprise Department, Govt. of Assam has notified the “**Assam Logistics & Warehousing Policy of Assam, 2022”** on **20th August 2022**. This document provides the operational guidelines for applying incentives/subsidies offered under this policy.  
   2. These guidelines have been framed under the provisions of Clause \- 4.9 of the Assam Logistics & Warehousing Policy of Assam, 2022 with the objective of specifying procedures for applying for incentives, benefits, disbursement and for recovery of incentives if drawn irregularly or fraudulently. The period of validity and effective date of these guidelines shall be effective from **20th August 2022** and shall remain in force for a period of **5 (five)** years or until further orders.

2. # **Applicability :**

   1. Incentives under the policy shall be applicable to the Proprietorial firm, Partnership firm, Cooperative Societies, Limited liability Companies (LLP), Private Limited Company, Limited Company, Trust, Non-Government Organization (NGO), FPO/FPC as well as such infrastructure created by State Government/ SPSUs.

   2. Central Government agency/ Public sector undertaking shall not be eligible for any incentives under the Policy.

   3. Units availing incentives under this policy are also entitled to avail incentives/ benefits of other State/ Central sector schemes/ policies providing the same kind of benefits/ Incentives.

3. # **Definitions and Eligibility**

   1. ### **Integrated/ Multi Modal Logistics Park (MMLP):**

      An Integrated/ Multi Modal Logistics Park (MMLP) is defined as a multi-modal freight and link facility with a minimum area of 75 acres with a total investment of at least 75 Crores, comprising mechanized warehouses, specialized storage solutions such as cold storage, facilities for mechanized material handling and inter-modal transfers container terminals, bulk/ break-bulk cargo terminals. Multimodal logistics parks are expected to provide the following key services:

1. Freight aggregation and distribution

   2. Multimodal freight transportation

      3. Inland Container Depot (ICD)

      4. Storage and Warehousing

      5. Value added services etc.

      MMLPs developed under this policy mandate that the developer allows other logistics operations to operate from the MMLP i.e., any player willing to pay terminal usage charge and other additional charges specified by the operator shall be allowed to avail the logistics services provided.

      MMLP is a logistics facility with access to more than one mode of transport. This allows it to serve as a point of inter modal change and will help drive higher usage of rail, coastal shipping, inland waterway, and higher capacity trucks for long haul. Integrated/ Multi Modal Logistics Park (MMLP) shall have the following provision:

1. MMLP should be accessible, at minimum, by a 2-lane paved road, with a minimum approach road of 60 ft

2. MMLP should have access to a rail siding. The development of the rail siding should be completed within three years of initiating the MMLP development

   2. ### **Warehouses:**

      A warehouse can be defined as any premise (including any protected place) which includes a place for storage and/or accumulation of goods under controlled conditions and equipped for providing handling, transportation as well as value added services such as sorting, grading, packaging etc. A warehouse may be characterized as Integrated Inland Container Depots/ Custom Bounded Areas, Warehouse for Agriculture Produces/ Grain Godowns, Warehouse other than agriculture produces. For this policy, a warehouse storage system may include but not be limited to the following:

      1. Warehousing complex with fixed facilities, moving units, rolling stocks.

      2. Open and closed storage, ambient condition storage for transit period.

      3. Fulfilment centres (in case of e-retailing), distribution centres.

3. ### **Logistic Park:**

   A logistic park should have contiguous stretch of minimum 30 acre of land for development, with minimum built up area of 3,00,000 sq. ft. The minimum fixed capital investment should be 30 Cr for development of infrastructure in the Logistic Park. A logistics park will include but not be limited to the following activities:

   1. Warehousing storage system

      2. Modern Weighting Facilities

      3. Industrial plots and ready to move in sheds

      4. Logistic services

      5. 	Infrastructure for value added and ancillary services, commercial activity  
      6. Sector specific in-bound and out-bound logistic

      7. Inter-modal transfer arrangements / facilities, truck terminals.

   4. ### **Container Freight Station/Inland Container Depot:**

      For Container Freight Station (CFS) / Inland Container Depot (ICD), minimum land area should be 10 acres with minimum investment of 30 Cr.

   5. ### **Integrated Cold Chain:**

      For creation of Integrated Cold Chain facility, IQF, Irradiation, VHT, laboratory minimum land area should be 1 acre with minimum investment of 10 Cr.

   6. ### **Primary Processing Centre (PPC):**

      For creation of Primary Processing Centre (PPC) land area should be minimum of 1 bigha with minimum investment of 1 Cr.

   7. ### **Cargo Electric bicycle:**

      A vehicle will be classified as an ‘Cargo electric bicycle’ if it is an electrically assisted pedal cycle and if the below specified conditions are met:

      1. Vehicle is equipped with an electric motor having power less than 0.25KW; whose output is progressively reduced and finally cut off as the vehicle reaches a speed of 25 kmph, or sooner, if the cyclist stops pedalling  
      2. Maximum speed is less than 25 kmph

      3. Vehicle is fitted with suitable breaks and retro reflective devices

      4. The vehicle should be either 2-wheeled or 3-wheeled

5. The vehicle should have a factory fitted front or rear or both cargo carrying racks/carriers with a minimum load carrying capacity of 60 Kg (excluding weight of cyclist and the vehicle)

   8. ### **Fixed Capital Investment:**

      Fixed Capital Investment means and includes investment in plant and machinery and land and building connected directly for the infrastructure. The investment in land shall mean the cost of land (used only for utilities) derived from registration value of land or the actual cost of the land, whichever is lower.

   9. ### **Project Appraisal Report of Bank/ Financial Institution:**

      The applicant unit should have loan component of minimum 50% of the project cost from any Bank or Financial Institution and should provide the project appraisal of Bank/Financial Institution to competent authority.

4. # **Components which will be considered for computing value of Fixed Capital Investment:**

   1. Civil Construction works directly related to the development of Logistics Park, MMLP, Warehouse, Container Freight Station/ Inland Container Depot, Cold Chain facility, Primary Processing Centre restricted to Schedule of Rates followed by APWD (B).  
   2. Freight charges paid for bringing Plant & Machinery and equipment from supplier’s premises to the location of the unit.  
   3. Storage and handling equipment including equipment like forklift, hydraulic trolley, and any other handling equipment used inside the factory premises.  
   4. Lift/ Elevator/ Escalator used for movement of Goods only.

   5. Electrical components necessary for plant operation.

   6. Firefighting equipment.

   7. Electric pump and motors.

   8. Explosive Detection Machine.

   9. Security Alarm System, CCTV.

   10. Weighbridge, Weighing Scale.

   11. Any other plant and machinery that is critical and directly connected to development of facility as given in Clause 3 of this guideline.

5. # **Components which will not be considered for computing value of Fixed Capital Investment:**

   1. Cost of development of land, preliminary and pre-operative expenses, interest during the period, consultant fees, margin money for working capital shall not be considered as fixed capital investment for the purpose of calculating subsidy.

   2. If a unit avails a particular incentive/ subsidy under this policy, the same entity for the same project is not allowed to avail that incentive/ subsidy under any other scheme/policy. Example: If Capital Subsidy is availed under any other policy/scheme the unit cannot avail Capital Subsidy under this policy.

   3. Loading and unloading charges

   4. Commissioning cost

   5. Captive Power Plants

   6. Boundary wall and gate, Approach Road/internal road, Land development Used machinery/ equipment/ materials for facility  
   7. MMLP, Logistics Park, Warehouses in Kamrup Metro District are ineligible for Capital Subsidy.

6. # **Pre-Registration under the Policy:**

   1. The unit should apply for pre-registration online as per **Form A1** to the Commissioner of Industries & Commerce, Assam through Single Window Portal, accessible through the URL [https://eodb.assam.gov.in](https://eodb.assam.gov.in/) before Commencement of Commercial Operation.

   2. Commissioner of Industries & Commerce, Assam will depute suitable officers either from the Commissionerate of Industries & Commerce, Assam or District Industries & Commerce Centre to make a physical inspection of the proposed unit within 15 days of receipt of the application and the concerned officer will furnish report to the Commissioner of Industries & Commerce, Assam as per **Form A1.1**  
   3. Commissioner of Industries & Commerce, Assam will be the competent authority to issue a Registration Certificate.  
   4. The Registration Certificate will be issued on First Come First Serve basis and will be restricted to the target as envisaged in the Policy.

7. # **Claims under the policy	:**

   1. Claims under the Policy will have to be submitted within 1 (One) Year of Commencement of Commercial Operation.

   2. Assessment of Civil construction shall be computed as per the “Schedule of Rates” followed by APWD and should be compared with the Bank Appraisal, CA Certificate, and actual expenditure statement of the unit. Any payment made by cash shall be deducted.

   3. The cut-off date for accounting investment in Civil Construction as well as in plant & machinery for the purpose of subsidy shall be the date of Commencement of Commercial Operation as indicated in UDYAM/ IEM-B as applicable.

   4. Commissionerate of Industries & Commerce, Assam shall release the fund through Direct Beneficiary Transfer (DBT) against the incentive/s approved by the SLEC subject to Sanction of fund by the Government.

   5. In the event of any irregularities/ discrepancies or misrepresentation of facts, Commissionerate of Industries & Commerce, Assam will issue Notice/s, to withdraw the benefits. The concerned authority may allow the unit to submit their representation/ replies within 15 (fifteen) days from the date of issuance of Notice/s.

   6. In the event of closure of any unit after functioning for five years from the date of Commissioning, the amount of subsidy so approved by the concerned committee shall be released to the unit concerned through their Bank/s or Financial Institution/s. However, such units will have to produce supporting documents pertaining to the functionality of the unit for the last five years such as electricity bill, financial statements (audited annual balance sheets, GST certificate)

   7. While implementing the policy, if any of the clause/ wordings in the Policy, Form/s need modification or alteration which may be addition of a new clause/ para/ new column/row or deletion, Industries, Commerce & Public Enterprise Department, Govt. of Assam will be the Competent Authority to do so.

9. # **Submission of various claims and incentives**

   1. Registered units will have to click on the Registration Number to apply online for availing various incentives under the Policy through the website [https://eodb.assam.gov.in.](https://eodb.assam.gov.in/)

   2. Application for Infrastructure Development related incentives viz. \- Capital Subsidy as per Clause 6.1 of the Policy will be submitted as per **Form- A2** along with documents as per check list. Enquiry report for Capital Subsidy will be as per **Form A-2.1**

   3. Application for Capital Subsidy for Refrigerated Vehicles incentives as per Clause 6.1 of the Policy will be submitted as per **Form- A3** along with documents as per check list. Enquiry report for Capital Subsidy for refrigerated vehicle incentives will be as per Form A-3.1

   4. Application for Stamp Duty Reimbursement as per Clause 6.2 of the Policy will be submitted as per **Form- A4** along with documents as per check list. Enquiry report for Stamp Duty Reimbursement will be as per **Form A-4.1**

   5. Application for Capacity Building incentives as per Clause 6.3 of the Policy will be submitted as per **Form- A5** along with documents as per check list.

   6. Application for Start-up incentives for research & innovation in logistic sector as per Clause 6.4 of the Policy will be submitted as per **Form- A6** along with documents as per check list. Enquiry report for Start-up incentives for research & innovation in logistic sector will be as per **Form A-6.1**

   7. Application for Green Logistics: Promotion of Electric Bicycles in for last mile deliveries in cities as per Clause 6.5 Policy will be submitted as per **Form- A7** along with documents as per check list.

   8. Application for Green Logistics: Hybrid or Plug-in Electric or Electric Cargo Vehicles incentives as per Clause 6.6 of the Policy will be submitted as per **Form- A8** along with documents as per check list.

   9. Procedure for claiming 50% exemption of registration charges and 100% road tax exemption: On production of details of payment of Electric Vehicle in Assam by the resident of Assam, District Transport Officers (DTO) will allow 50% exemption in registration fee of the vehicle and 100% road tax exemption. The dealers will be allowed to register the Electric Vehicle with 50% exemption in registration fee if any resident of Assam procures the Vehicle.

10. Government of Assam will float bids for private sector shippers from time to time to incentivize cargo vessel movement through Viability Gap Funding
