# **OPERATIONAL GUIDELINES OF** **THE ASSAM BAMBOO AND CANE POLICY, 2019**

1. # **Introduction:**

   These operational guidelines have been prepared for implementation of The Assam Bamboo and Cane Policy, 2019, as per sub clause **(f)** under **Additional Incentives** under clause **XI** of The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy, 2019\.

   The interventions under The National Bamboo Mission, shall be guided by the revised Operational Guidelines of The National Bamboo Mission and/or the rules and regulations, and the definitions thereof formulated by the mission from time to time. The revised Operational Guidelines can be downloaded from [www.nbm.nic.in](http://www.nbm.nic.in/)

2. # **General Provisions:**

* The guidelines covers the project/services which commence Commercial Production or the existing units which go in or substantial expansion/modernization/diversification on or after **1/1/2020** but on or before **31/12/2024** (inclusive of both the dates) or till the time the Government of Assam deems fit, with such further modifications as may be brought about during the operation of this policy.  
* The unit will have to apply within 1 (one) year after the date of Commercial Production to avail any component of the subsidy.  
* In case of any variance of any definitions/rules etc between The National Bamboo Mission and The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy 2019, the definitions/rules etc framed under The National Bamboo Mission shall prevail for the incentives to be provided under The National Bamboo Mission.  
* In case of any variance of any definitions/rules etc between The National Bamboo Mission and The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy 2019, the definitions/rules etc framed under The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy 2019, shall prevail for the additional incentives to be provided under The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy 2019\.  
* The State Bamboo Development Agency shall have the right to ask for any documents to ascertain the eligibility and authenticity of a unit before release of any subsidy.  
* The Financial Incentives to be provided under The National Bamboo Mission will be through Credit Linked Back Ended Subsidy as per the terms of The Revised National Bamboo Mission Operational Guidelines.

3. # **Definitions:**

1. **The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy, 2019:** The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy, 2019 means, the policy enunciated by the Government of Assam vide Notification No. CI.227/2019/61 Dated Dispur the 28th November, 2019\.

2. **National Bamboo Mission:** The National Bamboo Mission means The restructured National Bamboo Mission, that was approved by The Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs on 25th April. 2018\.  
3. **Fixed Capital Investment:** Fixed Capital Investment means and includes investment in plant and machinery and land and building connected directly for the infrastructure. The investment in land shall mean the cost of land (used only for utilities) derived from registration value of land or the actual cost of the land, whichever is lower. While the proportionate stamp duty and registration fee shall for the part of the cost of the land. The cost of development of land, preliminary and pre-operative expenses, interest during the construction period, consultant fees, margin money for working capital shall not be considered as fixed capital investment in the purpose of calculating subsidy.

4. **Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises:** Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises means enterprises as per investment criteria defined under the Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development Act 2006 or as amended.

5. **Substantial Expansion**: It means increase in value of Fixed Capital Investment of an existing unit by at least 25% as well as increase of employment by at least 10%. Prior to going for expansion, the unit should be operating at least at an average 75% capacity during the period of 3 previous years.  
6. **Diversification** means Fixed Capital Investment made by an existing unit for manufacturing of new product(s)/for rendering new service(s) in the same location of the existing unit, provided that the Additional Fixed Capital Investment is not less than 25% of the gross value of existing Fixed Capital investment and increase in regular employment at least by 10% of people of Assam.  
7. **Modernisation** means investment made by an existing unit in the Fixed Capital Investment in the same location of the existing unit, involving new/improved technology having definite advantage(s) in the same location of the existing unit in reduction of cost of production or in increase of profit margin, provided that the additional Fixed Capital Investment is not less than 25% of the gross Fixed Capital Investment.  
8. **Implementing Agency:** State Bamboo Development Agency, Assam shall be the implementing agency for The Assam Bamboo and Cane Policy, 2019\.  
9. **Souvenir Shops:** Souvenir Shops means a store primarily selling souvenirs and other items exclusively related to bamboo and cane.  
10. **Places of Tourist Attraction:** Places of Tourist Attraction means a tourist attraction is a place of interest where tourists visit, typically for its inherent or an exhibited natural or cultural value, historical significance, natural or built beauty, offering leisure and amusement. The places may include Kamakhya Temple, National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries of Assam, Talatal Ghar, Rang Ghar etc. The list is only indicative and not exhaustive.

4. # **Eligibility Criteria:**

1. A unit shall have employment of minimum of 80% people of Assam in the Managerial cadre and minimum of 90% people of Assam in the Non- Managerial cadre. Employment certificate for Micro and Small units shall be issued by the District Employment Officer of the concerned district. Employment certificate for Medium and Large units shall be issued by the Director of Employment & Craftsmen Training.  
2. The applicants/artisans/entrepreneurs shall not be eligible for any component of subsidy/subsidies under this policy if they have availed a similar subsidy/subsidies from any other scheme/policy of Govt of India and Assam.  
3. The unit undergoing Expansion/Modernization/ Diversification shall be eligible for incentives only for the Additional Fixed Capital Investment made

   i.e. for the Expanded/ Diversified/ Modernized portion of the existing unit.

4. No right or claim for any incentives under the Policy shall be deemed to have been conferred by the policy merely by virtue of the fact that the unit has fulfilled on its part the conditions of the Policy. The incentives/subsidies cannot be claimed as a matter of right.

5. The decision of the implementing agency, subject to such direction as Government may issue from time to time in this regard shall be final and binding.

**CAPITAL INVESTMENT SUBSIDY**

1. ###  **Objective:**

   The objective of this component is to encourage the bamboo artisans to buy state of the art plant and machinery and thereby promoting new and innovative bamboo products.

   2. ###  **Pattern of Assistance:**

   This component shall be reimbursed @ 50% of the eligible plant and machineries and internal electrical installations subject to a ceiling of Rs. 5 (five) crore.

   3. ###  **Eligibility:**

   The following components will be included for computing **the value of plant and machineries:**

1. Cost of Industrial Plant & Machinery including taxes and duties.

2. Cost of productive equipment’s such as tools, jigs, dyes and moulds including taxes and duties.  
3. Freight charges paid for bringing Plant & Machinery and equipment from suppliers premises to the location of the unit  
4. Transit insurance premium paid. Erection and installation charges to be restricted to the cost indicated in the Appraisal Note of the Financial Institution which provided loan to the unit.  
5. Laboratory testing equipments to be restricted to the cost indicated in the Appraisal Note of the Financial Institution which provided loan to the unit.  
6. Internal Electrification directly related to Plant & Machineries for Production. The following components will not be included for computing **the value of plant and machineries:**  
1. Loading and unloading charges.

2. Miscellaneous fixed assets such as DG Sets, handling equipments, electrical components other than Electrical components necessary for plant operation on the plant side from where meter is installed up to the point where finished goods is to be produced/dispatched (i.e. H.T. motors, L.T. motors, Switch boards, Panels, Capacitors, Relay, Circuit Breakers, Panel Boards, Switchgears.

4. Transformer

    **Bamboo & Cane Sick Industries Revival Scheme under The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy, 2019 (BSIRS)**

1. This scheme is prepared under Clause 7 (b) of The Assam Bamboo & Cane Policy, 2019\.

2. ### **Application of the scheme:**

   The scheme shall apply to only bamboo & cane units that are registered with the Department of Industries & Commerce, Assam.

3. ### **Definition:**

   The definitions on sickness which are attributed and in concurrence with the guidelines issued by the Reserve Bank of India, will automatically apply to this scheme.

4. ### **Incentives Under The Scheme:**

   1) A revival fund shall be shall be set up with necessary budgetary allocation for partially compensating the sacrifices made by the Financial Institutions, Banks etc. The fund shall be kept at the disposal of the Commissioner, Industries & Commerce and shall be operated by him. The number of units to be provided relief shall be restricted to the funds available in any of the financial year  
   2) 5% interest subsidy on Working Capital credit will be provided to all identified/eligible sick units, subject to maximum of Rs.2.00 lakhs per unit per year for a maximum period of five years from the date of sanction of additional working capital after declaration of the unit as sick. However, the component of the interest to be reimbursed shall be projected by the financing bank to the State Level Committee after following the guidelines. Once the State Level Committee accepts the proposal, the Commissioner of Industries shall draw the eligible interest subsidy on annual basis and reimburse to the Financing Bank.

5. ### **Procedure for sanction of interest reimbursement to Banks**:

   After the approval by the State Level Committee, the Commissioner of Industries & Commerce shall draw the eligible interest subsidy from BSIRS Fund kept at his disposal and pay the same to the Bank/financial institution concerned by way of a Demand Draft, subject to the following guidelines:-

   1) For the Working Capital Loan, bank will work out interest based on availing the loans by the unit on quarterly/annual basis and the first 50% eligible amount can be released only after submission of Interest Payment Certificate by the bank.

   2) The second installment i.e. the balance 50% of the 5% interest rebate eligible for the first financial year shall be drawn after completion of 12 months period of successful running of the Unit and on receipt of a certificate from the financial institution as per the format enclosed.  
   3) 	If the performance of the unit is found not satisfactory to the financial institution even after the completion of 12 months from the date of sanction, the unit is not eligible for release of the 2nd installment of interest rebate.

   4) The financial Institution/Bank is at liberty to submit a detailed report to CI\&C regarding unsatisfactory performance of the unit with reasons to enable the later to stop all further concessions under the BSIRS.

6. ### **Checklist for application:**

   1) Registration certificate of the unit.

   2) Udyam Registration.

   3) List of Directors/Partners

   4) Brief Note on the reason for sickness and potential of the firm to turnaround performance  
   5) Revenue projections of the firm for the next 3 years.

   6) Audited accounts for the preceding 3 years.

   7) Any	other	documents/documents	as	and	when	required	by	Commissioner,

      Industries/Banks

   8) Detail revival plan of the unit

7. ### **Procedure for application by the concerned sick industries:**

   1) The sick units shall apply in the concerned application form along with all the documents as per check list.  
   2) The application form is scrutinized as per the guidelines by SBDA, Assam.

   3) SBDA, Assam will then forward the application to the concerned bank and the bank shall carry out an exercise regarding revival of the unit as per the guidelines of the scheme and send a report of the same to SBDA, Assam within 30 days.  
   4) The sanction orders of the bank shall include the details of the amounts sanctioned towards working capital. The Bank shall also indicate the number of installments in which the amount so sanctioned would be released giving the details of amount and date of release of each installment. It shall also give the rate of interest at which the package is sanctioned and the actual interest amount due in the first 12 months from the date of release of the first installment together with the interest amount being claimed as rebate for the first year from Commissioner of Industries.  
   5) SBDA, Assam shall then place the report with the State Level Committee (SLC) for consideration and approval.  
   6) The State Level Committee shall examine the report of the financial institution and the appraisal report submitted by the bank to ensure that it confirms to the guidelines laid down in the scheme and it gives its approval.  
   7) Upon approval, SBDA Assam shall place the necessary amount of interest subsidy with the bank and the bank shall sanction and disburse the additional loan amount to the unit.  
   8) Interest dues on cash credit or Term Loan and panel rate of interest will be waived off from the amounting year of the unit in which it started incurring cash loss continuously.

8. ### **Terms and Conditions for relief:**

   1) The industrial units shall have to remain in production continuously at least till the expiry of the rehabilitation period granted under the scheme.  
   2) The industrial unit shall furnish details regarding production, employment or any other information that the state Government and the SLC may require from time to time.
